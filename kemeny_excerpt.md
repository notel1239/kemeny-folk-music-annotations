# Excerpt from the Autobiography of John Kemeny

Chapter 12: On the Festivities in Marosszék

...The winter's hold was broken by a great feast to celebrate the conclusion of the harvest tax. The people from the surrounding villages gathered in the central square. The air was filled with the sound of a furulya, its high, piercing melody calling the dancers to form a circle. I observed the men and women begin their vigorous steps, performing what they called the 'verbunk'. Later in the evening, as the firelight grew, a slower, more solemn tune was played on a koboz, and an elder began to sing a historical lament about the days of King Matthias. The entire affair was a potent display of the region's spirit, far removed from the formal dances of the Gyulafehérvár court...
